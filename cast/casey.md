# Casey: the Retro Gamer

*Wants authentic Ad Lib Gold sound on a Windows 9x machine.*

**Modality: embodied and by-ear.** Runs Windows 95, 98, or ME on period hardware
or an emulator. Installs a driver from its INF, starts a game, and judges the
result by listening. Knows the hardware lore of the era but not kernel internals;
patient with tinkering, and reads a README or a forum thread long before any
source.

- **Goals:** hear the card's FM and wavetable voices in 9x games; install the
  driver from its INF with no build toolchain; know which games and modes the card
  supports; keep an old machine or an emulator stable while playing.
- **Frustrations:** a driver that assumes NT and skips 9x; a card that is detected
  yet stays silent; an install that needs a compiler or a signed package; guidance
  pitched at developers rather than players.
- **Works by:** installing the INF, launching a game, listening, and reporting
  what worked on which hardware. A kernel trace is out of reach, so plain guidance
  is what has to land.
